MUNNAR

Munnar

Munnar is a famous hill station located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, known for its stunning natural beauty and tranquil environment. It is situated at an elevation of about 1,600 to 1,700 meters above sea level, named after the confluence of three rivers—Muthirapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala ("Munnar" means "three rivers" in Malayalam). MUSSOORIE

Mussoorie

Mussoorie, often called the "Queen of Hills," is a charming and popular hill station located in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, India. It is perched at an altitude of approximately 2,000 meters (6,560 feet), offering breathtaking views of the Himalayas and the Doon Valley below. This “Queen of the Hills” is known for colonial charm, waterfalls, and panoramic hill views.

NAINITAL

Nainital

Nainital is a popular hill station located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India, situated at an altitude of approximately 2,084 meters (6,837 feet) above sea level. It is known as the "Lake City" due to the central presence of the eye-shaped Naini Lake, which is about two miles in circumference and surrounded by hills and mountains including Naina Peak, Deopatha, and Ayarpatha.

SHILLONG

Shillong

Shillong is a picturesque hill station and the capital city of Meghalaya, located in the northeastern part of India. Situated on the Shillong Plateau at an elevation of approximately 1,520 meters (4,990 feet) above sea level, it is often referred to as the "Scotland of the East" due to its rolling hills, lush green landscapes, and climatic resemblance to the Scottish Highlands.

DARJEELING

Darjeeling

Famous for tea plantations and the historic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, it offers exceptional views of Kanchenjunga. Darjeeling is a renowned hill station located in the northern part of West Bengal, India, nestled in the Eastern Himalayas at an average elevation of about 2,045 meters (6,709 feet). Known as the "Queen of the Hills," Darjeeling is famous worldwide for its breathtaking views of the snow-capped Kanchenjunga, the third highest mountain in the world.

MANALI

Manali

Manali is a picturesque hill station located in the Kullu district of Himachal Pradesh, India. It is situated at an elevation of approximately 2,625 meters (8,612 feet) in the northern part of the Kullu Valley, surrounded by majestic Himalayan mountains. Manali is about 270 kilometers north of the state capital Shimla and roughly 544 kilometers northeast of Chandigarh.

St. MORTIZ

St. Mortiz

St. Moritz is a prestigious high Alpine resort town located in the Engadine valley in the Swiss canton of Graubünden, Switzerland. Positioned at an elevation of about 1,800 meters (5,910 feet) above sea level, it is renowned as one of the world’s most famous winter sports destinations and a luxury holiday spot.It has a reputation for elegance and world-class hospitality, attracting celebrities and elite travelers.

QUEENSTOWN

Queenstown

Queenstown is a resort town located in the Otago region on the South Island of New Zealand. It is situated on the northwestern shore of Lake Wakatipu, a long, S-shaped glacial lake, and is surrounded by dramatic mountain ranges including The Remarkables, Cecil Peak, and Ben Lomond. The town lies at an elevation of about 310 meters (1,020 feet) above sea level, offering stunning views of the lake and nearby mountains.

BANFF

Banff

Banff National Park is Canada’s oldest national park, established in 1885, and is located in the Canadian Rockies of Alberta. It spans approximately 6,641 square kilometers (2,564 square miles) of mountainous terrain, including glaciers, ice fields, dense coniferous forests, alpine meadows, and pristine lakes. The park lies about 110-180 kilometers (68-112 miles) west of Calgary, along the Trans-Canada Highway.
